default.

restoring the body's basic functions

WHAT

Default is not a new method.

It is a return to what the body already knows before comfort, inactivity, and habits reduce its capabilities.

It does not train isolated muscles.

It restores integrated functions.

Strength, mobility, coordination, balance, breathing work together, as they should.

HOW

Sessions last no more than 45 minutes.

No session is ever the same. Exercises may look similar, intensity adapts to the person.

The work is based on:

  • time under tension (TUT)
  • quality of execution (TUA)
  • nasal breathing only
  • control before fatigue

Timed sets are containers. What happens inside — quality, focus, control — determines the work.

The last repetition must be equal to or better than the first.

PRACTICE

Work happens on the ground and standing.

In quadruped positions. In suspension.

With bodyweight and minimal tools — rings, rope, kettlebell.

Slow movements. Explosive movements. Continuous movement.

Strength, mobility, and flow in the same session.

Some days you push. Some days you explore. Some days you recover by moving.

SELF-ASSESSMENT

Default requires attention.

You do not follow instructions blindly.

You learn to assess:

  • how you breathe
  • how you move
  • when to stop
  • when to push

The goal is not endurance.

The goal is sensing what works and what doesn’t.

WHO

Default is for people who:

  • have experienced pain or stiffness
  • value quality over quantity
  • want to move better, for longer

It is not for those who:

  • look for shortcuts
  • just want to sweat
  • delegate everything to someone else
